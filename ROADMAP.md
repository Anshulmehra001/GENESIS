## Phase 2: Evolution & Complexity

**Timeline:** Weeks 3-6
**Status:** ðŸ”œ NEXT

### Goals

Transform simple organisms into complex beings with:
- Neural network brains (not just simple parameters)
- Predator-prey dynamics (food chains)
- Communication (organisms talk to each other)
- Memory (remember past experiences)
- Advanced sensing (see farther, detect others)

### Key Features to Implement

1. **Neural Network Genomes**
   - Replace simple parameters with neural networks
   - Inputs: sensory data (energy, organisms, position)
   - Outputs: actions (move, eat, reproduce, signal)
   - Evolution of network weights and topology

2. **Predator-Prey System**
   - Predators hunt other organisms
   - Prey flee from predators
   - Population cycles (boom and bust)
   - Co-evolution (arms race)

3. **Communication**
   - Organisms emit signals (alarm, food, mating)
   - Signals propagate through space
   - Organisms respond to signals
   - Signal evolution (new meanings emerge)

4. **Memory System**
   - Remember high-energy locations
   - Avoid dangerous areas
   - Recognize individuals
   - Learn from experience

5. **Advanced Behaviors**
   - Territorial behavior
   - Migration patterns
   - Risk assessment
   - Energy conservation strategies

### Expected Outcomes

- Ecological niches emerge
- Multiple species coexist
- Complex food webs form
- Behavioral diversity increases
- Stable ecosystems develop

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Predator-prey cycles observed
- [ ] Communication protocols emerge
- [ ] Multiple ecological niches occupied
